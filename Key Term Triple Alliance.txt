Key Term: Triple Alliancea treaty of alliance between Germany, Austria-Hungary, and ItalyKey Term: Triple Ententea treaty of alliance between France, Russia, and BritainKey Term: Schlieffen Plana German war plan to sweep through Belgium and northernFrance before turning to RussiaKey Term: Balkan Leaguean alliance created in 1912 by Greece, Montenegro, Bulgaria,and Serbia against the Ottoman EmpireKey Term: Alliesthe nations that united to oppose Germany and Austria-Hungary, originally, Russia, France, andBritainKey Term: Central powersa World War I coalition that included the Austro-Hungarian, German, and Ottoman EmpiresKey Term: total wara war fought using all available resources, with no restrictions on weapons ortheir targetsKey Term: U-boatsGerman submarines equipped with torpedoes that sank thousands of pounds of cargo over the course of World War IKey Term: Zimmermann Telegrama 1917 telegram sent by Germany’s foreign minister offering an alliance with Mexico in return for Mexico causingdisturbances along its U.S. borderKey Term: Sykes-Picot Agreementa secret agreement reached between France andBritain in 1916 to partition areas of the Middle Eastafter the warKey Term: War Industries Board (WIB)a U.S. federal agency created in 1917 to control the economic andindustrial output of factories in times of warKey Term: Women’s Land icya British program to help women ensure enough foodstuffs were produced on farms while men servedin the militaryKey Term: Espionage Acta 1917 act passed in the United States that made anti-war propaganda illegalKey Term: Sedition Acta 1918 act passed in the United States that forbade forms of speech considered disloyal to the war effortKey Term: Easter Risingthe 1916 rebellion of Irish Nationalists against the British in DublinKey Term: Bolsheviksa radical majority faction of Russia’s Social Democratic Party led byVladimir LeninKey Term: armisticea cease-fire agreement
